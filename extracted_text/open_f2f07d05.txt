Pakistan Economic Survey FY23
Key Highlights
Pakistan’s GDP grew by 0.29% in FY23 (FY22: 6.1%).
Services sector growth was recorded at 0.86% in FY23 vs 6.19% in FY22 with Wholesale &
Retail trade posting a decline of 4.46% during the year (FY22: 10.3%).
Industry sector growth stood at -2.49% in FY23 vs 6.83% last year. Large-Scale Manufacturing
witnessed a decline of 8.11% during 9MFY23 (versus 10.61% during 9MFY22). Only four
sectors posted positive growth during the period including: Furniture, wearing apparels, other
manufacturing (footballs) and leather products.
Agriculture sector growth stood at 1.55% in FY23 vs 4.27% in FY22. The Livestock sector
growth of 3.78% was recorded, accompanied by forestry and fishing showing growth of 3.93%
and 1.44%, respectively.
The size of Pakistan’s economy (nominal GDP) increased to PKR 84.7trn in FY23 compared
to PKR 66.6trn recorded in FY22. In dollar terms, the size of the economy reached to USD
341.6bn.
During FY23, the Per Capita Income increased to PKR 388,755 (equivalent to USD 1,568.4).
The decline in GDP per capita (dollar terms) of 11.2% was mainly due to currency depreciation,
lower growth, and rising population.
Total investment during FY23 posted a growth of 10.2% (FY22: 29.1% YoY) marking 13.6 % of
GDP (FY22:15.7%).
Moreover, National Saving increased by 44.7% against a decline of 3.8% in last year, settling
at 12.6% of GDP (FY22: 11.1%).

Pakistan Economic Survey FY23
Key Highlights

Current Account Deficit narrowed down by 76% to USD 3.3bn versus USD 13.7bn in FY22,
owing mostly to lower imports. Exports witnessed decline of 12.1% to reach USD 25.4bn for
11MFY23 (compared to USD 28.9bn for 11MFY22) while total imports during 11MFY23
clocked at USD 51.2bn showing a decline of 29.2% YoY. With this the trade deficit in goods
and services shrank by 40.4% YoY to USD 25.8bn during 11MFY23.

FDI inflows decreased by 23.2% to USD 1.17bn in 10MFY23 as against USD 1.52bn last year,
with major investments from China (30%) and Japan (15%). Power sector attracted the highest
amount of FDIs (44%) followed by the Financial Business (24%).

Remittances saw a YoY decrease of 13% to USD 22.7bn during 10MFY23. Major
contributions during 9MFY23 came from Saudi Arabia - USD 4.9bn (23.9%), U.A.E - USD
4.3bn (17.5%), U.K - USD 3.0bn (14.9%), and USA- USD 2.3bn (11.1%).

SBP’s reserves depleted to USD 4.2bn by the end of Mar’23 mainly on the account of
amortization of official loans and liabilities.
Money Supply (M2) grew by 7.3% to PKR 2.03trn during Jul’22 to mid May’23, compared to
6.2% growth in same period last year.
Private sector credit witnessed a massive decline, clocking-in at PKR 75.4bn during Jul’22 to
mid May’23 compared to PKR 1.4trn same period last year.
With high revenue collection of PKR 6.94trn (18.1% higher YoY), the fiscal deficit was
contained at 3.6% of GDP during 9MFY23 against last year same period’s deficit of 3.9%.

Pakistan Economic Survey FY23
Key Highlights
Total expenditures during 9MFY23 grew by 18.7% to PKR 10.1trn (PKR 8.4trn in same period
last year) mainly on account of 25.3% higher current expenditure (PKR 9.2trn). This higher
current expenditure was on the back of 69.1% jump in markup payments amid higher policy
rates at the domestic and international levels as well as Rupee depreciation.
Primary Balance posted a surplus of PKR 503.8bn vs. a deficit of PKR 447.2bn (9MFY22).
Tax Collection grew by 16.5% while non-tax revenues increased by 25.5% in 9MFY23. For the
same period, FBR revenue collection target exceeded by 17.6% to PKR 5.2trn.
Policy rate currently stands at 21%, as opposed to 13.75% at the beginning of FY23, as a
result of contractionary measures to limit inflation.
Annual Inflation (CPI) averaged at 29.2% during 11MFY23 against 11.3% recorded in same
period last year. This high inflation was mainly due to depreciation in the exchange rate, supply
disruptions caused by flood damages, increased global food prices, and broader energy tariff
hikes.
Total public debt was recorded at PKR 59.2trn (end Mar’23), with domestic debt of PKR
35.1trn, and external public debt of PKR 24.2trn or USD 85.2bn. The Government also
managed to pay-off Treasury Bills amounting to PKR 527bn which led to a reduction of short-
term maturities and PKR 310bn against SBP debt.
The damage caused by flooding is estimated at PKR 3.2trn (USD 14.9bn) and the need for
rehabilitation of damages stands at PKR 3.5trn (USD 16.3bn).
